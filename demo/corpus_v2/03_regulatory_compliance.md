# Financial Regulatory Compliance Guide

Financial institutions operate within a complex web of regulations designed to protect consumers, maintain market integrity, and ensure systemic stability. This guide outlines the major regulatory frameworks and compliance requirements applicable to banks, broker-dealers, and fintech companies operating in the United States and European Union.

#### Key Regulatory Bodies

Understanding which regulators have jurisdiction over specific activities is the first step in building a compliance program.

| Regulator | Jurisdiction | Primary Focus |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|
| SEC | United States | Securities markets, investment advisers, public company disclosure |
| FINRA | United States | Broker-dealer conduct, market surveillance |
| OCC | United States | National bank supervision, safety and soundness |
| FCA | United Kingdom | Financial conduct, consumer protection |
| BaFin | Germany | Banking, insurance, securities supervision |
| EBA | European Union | Prudential regulation, stress testing coordination |

#### Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Requirements

AML compliance requires a risk-based approach encompassing customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting. The Bank Secrecy Act in the United States mandates that financial institutions file Currency Transaction Reports for cash transactions exceeding $10,000 and Suspicious Activity Reports when transactions appear to involve proceeds from illegal activity.

The European Union's Anti-Money Laundering Directives, now in their sixth iteration, require member states to maintain beneficial ownership registries and impose enhanced due diligence for high-risk jurisdictions identified by the Financial Action Task Force.

#### Know Your Customer (KYC) Framework

| KYC Level | Trigger | Documentation Required |
|-----------|---------|----------------------|
| Simplified Due Diligence | Low-risk customers, small accounts | Government-issued ID, basic address verification |
| Standard Due Diligence | Most retail and commercial customers | ID verification, source of funds, occupation details |
| Enhanced Due Diligence | PEPs, high-risk jurisdictions, complex structures | Detailed source of wealth, ongoing monitoring, senior management approval |

#### Capital Adequacy and Prudential Standards

Basel III requirements mandate minimum capital ratios including a Common Equity Tier 1 ratio of 4.5%, a Tier 1 capital ratio of 6%, and a total capital ratio of 8%. Additional buffers apply, including a capital conservation buffer of 2.5% and a countercyclical buffer of up to 2.5% depending on credit growth conditions.

Stress testing under the Comprehensive Capital Analysis and Review program requires large bank holding companies to demonstrate sufficient capital under severely adverse economic scenarios modeled by the Federal Reserve.

#### Compliance Program Elements

An effective compliance program must include written policies and procedures, a designated compliance officer with direct board access, regular risk assessments updated at least annually, employee training programs with documented completion records, independent testing and audit functions, and a system for tracking regulatory changes and implementing necessary updates. Institutions should adopt regtech solutions to automate monitoring where possible, reducing human error and improving detection rates.
